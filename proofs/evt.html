Extreme Value Theorem (Maximum)

Theorem: The Extreme Value Theorem (Maximum)

If \(K \subseteq \RR\), \(K\) is compact, \(f : K \lra \RR\), and \(f\) is continuous on \(K\), there exists \(b \in K\) such that, for all \(x \in K\), \(f(x) \leq f(b)\).

We rely on the following:

Lemma: Image is a Subset of the Codomain

If \(f : A \lra B\) and \(K \subseteq A\), then \(f(K) \subseteq B\).

Lemma: Function is Onto its Image

If \(f : A \lra B\), \(K \subseteq A\), and \(y \in f(K)\), then there exists \(x \in K\) such that \(f(x) = y\).

Lemma: Preservation of Compact Sets

If \(A \subseteq \RR\), \(f : A \lra \RR\), \(f\) is continuous on \(K\), \(K \subseteq A\), and \(K\) is compact, then \(f(K)\) is compact.

Lemma: Max of a Compact Set

If \(K \subseteq \RR\) and \(K\) is compact, then there exists \(b \in K\) such that for all \(x \in K\), \(x \leq b\).

Proof:

  1. Assume \(K \subseteq \RR\), \(K\) is compact, \(f : K \lra \RR\), and \(f\) is continuous on \(K\).
  2. By Preservation of Compact Sets, \(f(K)\) is compact.
  3. By Max of a Compact Set, I choose \(\beta \in f(K)\), such that for all \(y \in f(K)\), \(y \leq \beta\).
  4. Fix \(x \in K\).
  5. By Definition of Image, \(f(x) \in f(K)\)
  6. By Specification, \(f(x) \leq \beta\)
  7. By Function Onto its Image, I choose \(b \in K\), such that \(f(b) = \beta\).
  8. By Substitution, \(f(x) \leq f(b)\)
  9. By Generalization, for all \(x \in K\), \(f(x) \leq f(b)\).
  10. By Existence, there exists \(b \in K\) such that, for all \(x \in K\), \(f(x) \leq f(b)\).
  11. By direct proof, the theorem is proven.
