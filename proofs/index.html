The Rules of Inference

Deduction

Prerequisites:
  • if \(P\), then \(Q\) OR \(P\) iff \(Q\)
  • \(P\)
Result:
  • \(Q\)

The Rule of Direct Proof

Prerequisites:
  • A subproof from \(P\) to \(Q\)
We can:
  • Discharge \(P\)
  • If \(P\), then \(Q\)

The Rule of Generalization

Given that:
  • We have fixed \(x\) with some condition.
  • We have shown \(P(x)\).
  • There are no undischanrged assumptions about \(x\).
We can:
  • Deduce "For all \(x\) with the condition, \(P(x)\)".

The Rule of Specification

Given that:
  • We have shown "For all \(x\) with some condition, \(P(x)\)".
  • We have shown \(y\) has the condition.
We can:
  • Deduce \(P(y)\).
