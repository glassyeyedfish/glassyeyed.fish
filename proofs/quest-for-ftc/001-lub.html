The Least Upper Bound Property of the Real Numbers

Axiom: The Least Upper Bound Property of the Real Numbers

If \(A \subseteq \RR\) and \(A\) is bounded above, then there exists \(s \in \RR\) such that for all \(m \in \RR\), if \(m\) is an upper bound of \(A\), then \(s \leq m\).
