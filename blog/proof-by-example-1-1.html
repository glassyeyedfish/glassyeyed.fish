Proofs by Example: Example 1.1

In this series of articles the goal is simple. Teach you how to write math proofs using only a series of examples. This is because I think understanding examples and then practicinig exercieses are the best way to learn math (and anything really).

Let's not was time and get into the first example.

Example 1

Problem:

Prove that: For all \(n \in \ZZ\), if \(n\) is even, then \(n^2\) is even.

Solution:

Fix \(n \in \ZZ\). Assume \(n\) is even. By definition, this means that there exists \(k \in \ZZ\) such that \(n = 2k\). Using some algebra, we see that \( n^2 = (2k)^2 = 2(2k^2) \). I'll choose \( l = 2k^2 \). Thus, by substitution, \( n^2 = 2l \). By closure, we see that \( l \in \ZZ \). By existential introuction, we know that there exists \( l \in \ZZ \) such that \( n^2 = 2l \). By definition, that means \(n^2\) is even. By direct proof we obtain that if \(n\) is even, then \(n^2\) is even. By universal introduction, we conclude that for all \(n \in \NN\), if \(n\) is even, then \(n^2\) is even.

There is a lot to unpack here. For starters, if you didn't already know, this is the definition of an even numbers.

Definition: For all \(n \in \ZZ\), if \(n\) is even iff there exists \(k \in \ZZ\) such that \(n = 2k\).

Go back and read the proof again. Can you find the two places we use the definition of an even number? Just to make it clear, I will now show the proof again but with some highlights.

You'll that I have three colored highlights now. They all mean something specific!

  • A green statement is is a statement we have proven in the middle of our proof.
  • A blue stateemnt is a statement that a green statement follows from.
  • A purple statement is the justification for why a green statement follows from zero or more blue statements.

THIS IS VERY IMPORTANT! In every proof, pretty much every statement FOLLOWS from previous statements, and there is some JUSTIFICATION for why that is true. You should be able to analyze any proof and figure out what statements follow from what other statements and why.

The only exceptions are statements that we have declared to be true. In these articles, I will sometimes highlight them in orange. In our first example proof, here are the statements that have been declared instead of derived.
